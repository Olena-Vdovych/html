7 Essential Netherlands Travel Tips

But before visiting the country consisting Van Gogh's legendary masterpieces and before planning to capture those scenic beauties and the beaches a virtual exploration of the country is important. The Netherlands travel tips that I made to you may help you to plan a successful trip.

1. Carry Cash

With the advent of digitalization, we are now more dependent on our cards, whether it be a debit card or credit card. But while your visit to the Netherlands, do not simply rely on your cards. This is a basic travel tip in the Netherlands that always carry cash with you. In the Netherlands, they do love cards but only the Maestro Card. Contrasting with other countries in the worlds credit cards are not popular in the Netherlands. However, few of the stores are now going cashless but to be on the safer side carry cash with you.

2. Be An Expert In Time Management

It is important to manage time while visiting the Netherlands. If you need to go shopping or need to do any chore try to do it between 10 am and 5-6pm. Though few business shops are open till midnight, most of the businesses are shut down by 6 p.m and museums and shops remain closed on Mondays.

3. Be Specific While asking For Water

In the Netherlands asking for water is complex. You can get free water only if you know what to ask to get this otherwise you will be charged. If you are thirsty and you need water rather than asking simply for water move for asking a pitcher of tap water. Tap water is free here but if you simply ask for tap water you'll receive a very tiny amount of water and if you simply say water you'll get a non-refillable small bottle of water and they'll charge you money for the bottle. So for better move go for the pitcher.

4. Keep Yourself Ready with a Raincoat

Weather is unpredictable here. It is an important Netherlands travel tip to keep a raincoat with you. For all around the trip, a light raincoat and travel umbrella are valuable as rain, sun and cold can all happen on the same day. Raincoat does not make that much space in your luggage. So, you can bring one along instead of buying one from here. These days, you can easily find compact raincoats that fit in your back pocket. You can just take them out once it starts to rain.

5. The Netherlands have a Short Tulip Season

he Tulip period in the Netherlands is quite short. The window of Tulip flower blooming in the Netherlands is only 6 to 8 weeks and it is not same every year. It may get stretched or shorten. But if you are a Tulip lover then mid-April is the perfect time for you to visit the Netherlands and see the beautiful flower blooming. Unfortunately, if you miss the window then you should visit the globe's largest flower warehouse "FloraHolland" or Bloemenmarkt, Amsterdam to see or buy beautiful Tulips.

6. Get on to the Dutch Trains

The trains in the Netherlands are just great, feel free to use them quite often when you are travelling within the Netherlands. If you are on a budget than Dutch trains are your friends. It is better if you choose trains over cabs. You will save a lot of money and time The Netherlands is a small country; you can visit different places in the country on a train in a very short period of time.

7. Amsterdam is not the Whole Netherlands

One of the utmost travel tips to Amsterdam, Netherlands is that visit other places too in the Netherlands. By visiting only Chicago you cannot say you have seen America same as by just visiting Amsterdam you cannot say you have seen the Netherlands. Amsterdam has got crowded because of a large number of tourists travel there. As touring the Netherlands is quite cheaper you can visit other places like The Hague and Utrecht where you can experience the beautiful history of the country and most importantly you will get to visit the countryside and get a chance to look deeply into the Dutch culture.

Amsterdam

Amsterdam Canals:

Amsterdam is known for its beautiful canals, which were constructed in the 17th century to improve transportation and prevent flooding. The city has more than 100 kilometers of canals, with over 1,500 bridges, and the canal district is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Amsterdam Bicycles:

Amsterdam is one of the most bike-friendly cities in the world, with over 800,000 bicycles and more bikes than people. Cycling is a popular way to get around the city, and Amsterdam has a comprehensive network of bike lanes and bike parking facilities.

Amsterdam Red Light District:

Amsterdam's Red Light District is a famous tourist destination. The area is known for its sex shops, adult theaters, and prostitution. However, the district is also home to many restaurants, bars, and cultural attractions.

Amsterdam Museums:

Amsterdam has more than 50 museums, including the world-famous Rijksmuseum, Van Gogh Museum, and Anne Frank House. The city is also home to the Museumplein, a public square that houses many of the city's top museums.

Amsterdam Tulips:

Amsterdam is known for its beautiful tulips, which were introduced to the country in the 16th century. Every year, the city hosts the Keukenhof Gardens, which features over 7 million tulips and is one of the largest flower gardens in the world.

Amsterdam Coffee Shops:

Amsterdam is known for its liberal drug policies, and coffee shops in the city are allowed to sell small quantities of cannabis. However, it's important to note that smoking marijuana is only legal in designated areas and is not allowed in public.

Amsterdam Houseboats:

Amsterdam is famous for its houseboats, which are often used as homes or vacation rentals. The city has more than 2,500 houseboats, and many of them are located on the canals.

Hague

The Hague is:

is the third-largest city in the Netherlands, after Amsterdam and Rotterdam, with a population of around 540,000 people.

The Hague is:

home to many international organizations, including the International Court of Justice, the Permanent Court of Arbitration, and the International Criminal Court.

The Hague is:

also the seat of the Dutch government and the residence of the Dutch monarch, Queen Máxima and King Willem-Alexander. The official name of the city is 's-Gravenhage, which means "the counts' hedge."

The Hague is:

located on the western coast of the Netherlands, just a few kilometers from the North Sea. The city is known for its beautiful beaches and seaside resorts, including Scheveningen, which is one of the most popular seaside towns in the Netherlands.

The Hague is:

a hub of cultural and artistic activity in the Netherlands, with numerous museums and cultural institutions. Some of the most famous museums in the city include the Mauritshuis, which houses some of the world's most famous paintings, including Vermeer's "Girl with a Pearl Earring," and the Gemeentemuseum Den Haag, which is home to one of the largest collections of Mondrian's works in the world.

The Hague is:

also famous for its parks and gardens, including the Hague Forest, which is the largest urban forest in the Netherlands, and the Scheveningse Bosjes, which is a beautiful park located just a few kilometers from the city center.

The Hague is:

known for its traditional Dutch architecture, which can be seen in many of the city's historic buildings and landmarks. Some of the most famous landmarks in The Hague include the Binnenhof, which is a medieval castle that houses the Dutch parliament, and the Peace Palace, which is the headquarters of the International Court of Justice.

Leiden

Leiden is:

a city located in the province of South Holland in the Netherlands. It has a population of around 123,000 people.

Leiden is:

known for its rich history, which dates back to Roman times. It was also an important center of the Dutch Revolt against Spanish rule in the 16th century.

Leiden is:

Rembrandt van Rijn, one of the greatest Dutch painters of all time, was born in Leiden in 1606. The city has a museum dedicated to his life and work, the Rembrandt House Museum.

Leiden is:

famous for its canals, which are similar to those in Amsterdam. The city also has many beautiful parks and gardens, such as the Hortus Botanicus, which is one of the oldest botanical gardens in the world.

Leiden is:

On October 3rd of each year, Leiden celebrates the "Leidens Ontzet" (Relief of Leiden) festival, which commemorates the lifting of the Spanish siege of the city in 1574. The festival features parades, street fairs, and fireworks.

Leiden is:

Leiden is home to the oldest university in the Netherlands, Leiden University, which was founded in 1575. Many famous scholars and scientists, such as René Descartes and Albert Einstein, have studied or worked at the university.

Leiden is:

also home to the American Pilgrim Museum, which tells the story of the Pilgrims who lived in the city before sailing to America on the Mayflower in 1620.

North sea

North sea is:

a marginal sea of the Atlantic Ocean located between Great Britain, Scandinavia, and the European continent, including the Netherlands.

North sea is:

relatively shallow, with an average depth of only 94 meters. However, it has several deep trenches, including the Norwegian Trench, which is over 700 meters deep.

North sea is:

one of the busiest shipping lanes in the world, with more than 500 vessels passing through it every day. It is also a major fishing ground, with the Dutch fishing industry being a significant contributor to the local economy.

North sea is:

plays a vital role in the energy sector, with many offshore oil and gas platforms located in the region. The Netherlands has several large gas fields located under the North Sea, which have been an important source of energy for the country.

North sea is:

home to a diverse array of marine life, including several species of fish, seals, and birds. The Dutch coast is particularly important for migratory birds, which stop in the area during their annual journeys.

North sea is:

also known for its strong tides and currents, which make it a popular destination for water sports enthusiasts. Kite surfing, windsurfing, and sailing are all popular activities in the region.

North sea is:

The Dutch coast is known for its picturesque beaches and dunes, which attract millions of tourists every year. The beach town of Scheveningen is one of the most popular destinations, with a wide range of restaurants, cafes, and shops catering to visitors.
